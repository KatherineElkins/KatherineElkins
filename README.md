# Katherine Elkins


AI safety researcher and Co-Founder of the Human-Centered AI Lab. PI in the NIST AI Safety Institute Consortium. PI for a Schmidt Sciences HAVI grant building open-access AI tools for endangered cultural archives.
My research focuses on where language models break down — how they mishandle negation, respond to emotional manipulation, and fail at the kinds of prohibition and persuasion humans navigate every day. I build computational tools to find and measure these failures.
Current work:

LLM evaluation for the NIST AI Safety Institute Consortium (representing the Modern Language Association)
Archival Intelligence: AI tools for endangered archives in New Orleans (Schmidt Sciences HAVI)
SentimentArcs: computational methodology for emotional arc in narrative (95,000+ downloads, 198 countries)
Multi-agent behavioral simulation for high-stakes decision-making
